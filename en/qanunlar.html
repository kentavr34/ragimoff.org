Legal basis for practice

Holding a CPD diploma from a Russian educational institution, working as a psychologist in Holding a CPD diploma from a Russian educational institution, working as a psychologist in Azerbaijan is fully legal. Four official

The most common question: is diploma "nostrification" required?

Many people reasonably ask: "Is a Russian institute diploma valid in Azerbaijan, and does it require confirmation (nostrification) by the Ministry of Education?"

The answer: No, nostrification is not required — the activity is fully legal!
Unlike standard medical degrees, a diploma in continuing professional development (CPD) gives the legal right to work in the Republic of Azerbaijan without undergoing a confirmation procedure. Upon receiving the diploma, you may begin practice immediately.

This right is directly governed by the legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan and the decrees of the Cabinet of Ministers. Four official legal grounds are presented below.

Verify on the portal
1
Law of the Republic of Azerbaijan
Law "On Psychological Assistance" — who is a psychologist?
▾
Article 1.0.2 — Definition of "psychologist"

"A psychologist is a person who meets the requirements of this Law, who has received higher education in the specialty of psychologist or who has acquired the specialty of psychologist through additional education in accordance with the Law of the Republic of Azerbaijan 'On Education' and who has the right to provide psychological assistance."

Article 3.2 — Use of the word "psychologist"

"The use of the words 'psychologist', 'psychological', 'psychoanalysis', 'psychodiagnostics', 'psychotherapy'... is permitted only by persons who have the right to provide psychological assistance in accordance with this Law."

In other words: a person holding a continuing professional development (CPD) diploma is legally entitled to use the title "psychologist" and provide psychological assistance.

Read full text — e-qanun.az
2
Law of the Republic of Azerbaijan
Law "On Education" — what is additional education?
▾
Article 24 — Additional education

"Additional education is education provided on the basis of additional education programmes with the purpose of meeting the comprehensive educational needs of citizens."

This article recognises continuing professional development (CPD) as an official form of education. The diploma from the St Petersburg Institute of CPD falls precisely within this category.

Read full text — e-qanun.az
3
Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Azerbaijan
Decree No. 163 — procedure for professional retraining
▾
Clause 3.1.1 — Definition of retraining

"Retraining is an additional form of education that fulfils the aspiration of every citizen who has received a primary basic professional education to master a new profession or speciality in the relevant field and serves the more dynamic and flexible satisfaction of actual staffing needs."

A person with a higher education in another speciality may undergo retraining in psychology and receive a full psychology diploma. This is an official decree of the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

Read full text — e-qanun.az
4
International Agreement — AR Law No. 455-IIQ
"On Mutual Recognition of Education Documents, Academic Degrees and Academic Titles between the Government of the AR and the Government of the RF" — Baku, 13 May 2003
▾
LAW OF THE REPUBLIC OF AZERBAIJAN No. 455-IIQ · 13 May 2003

Under the Education Agreement between Russia and Azerbaijan, graduates holding a CPD diploma — provided they meet employer requirements — may officially practise in both public and private institutions.

This Agreement establishes the diploma from the St Petersburg Institute of Additional Professional Education as an officially recognised document in Azerbaijan.
